HTML Page Title

In HTML, the page title is defined within the <title> element, which is nested inside the <head> section of your HTML document. The page title is what appears in the browser's title bar or tab, and it is also used by search engines to display the title of the page in search results. Here’s how you can set the page title in your HTML document:

Example:

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
<head>
    <meta charset="UTF-8">
    <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">

    <!-- Page title -->
    <title>Your Page Title</title>

    <!-- Other meta tags, links to CSS, etc. -->
</head>
<body>
    <!-- Your page content -->
</body>
</html>

Explanation:

  • <title> Element: This element specifies the title of the HTML document. It should be placed inside the <head> section.
  • Text Content: Replace Your Page Title with the actual title of your web page. This title appears in the browser's title bar or tab.

Best Practices:

  1. Descriptive Titles: Choose a concise and descriptive title that accurately represents the content of the page.
  1. SEO Considerations: Include relevant keywords in your page title to improve search engine optimization (SEO).
  1. Unique Titles: Ensure each page on your website has a unique title to differentiate it from other pages.

Example of a More Detailed <head> Section:

<head>
    <meta charset="UTF-8">
    <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">

    <!-- Page title -->
    <title>Example Website - Home</title>

    <!-- Meta description for SEO -->
    <meta name="description" content="Welcome to Example Website. Learn about our products and services.">

    <!-- Link to CSS stylesheet -->
    <link rel="stylesheet" href="styles.css">

    <!-- Favicon -->
    <link rel="icon" href="favicon.ico" type="image/x-icon">

    <!-- Additional meta tags, scripts, etc. -->
</head>

Accessibility:

  • Ensure your page title accurately reflects the content of the page for users who rely on screen readers or other assistive technologies.

Testing:

  • Test your page title across different browsers and devices to ensure it displays correctly and is easily identifiable by users.

Conclusion:

Setting the <title> element in your HTML document is essential for defining the title of your web page. It not only appears in the browser's title bar or tab but also plays a significant role in SEO. By following best practices and ensuring your title is clear and relevant, you can enhance both user experience and discoverability of your web pages.
